# What a Company Is and What It Does

This chapter brief turns the existing company research into a narrative chapter centered on one claim: a company is a bounded system organized to create, deliver, and capture value. The chapter should move from the outer boundary of the company to its inner operating core, then close by connecting that model to the idea of one human coordinating many agents.

## Sections

### Chapter Purpose

The purpose of this chapter is to give the reader a stronger mental model of the company before introducing the future company. The chapter should prevent the reader from reducing the company to a legal shell, a team, or a product, and instead show the company as a multi-layered system with direction, boundary, control, execution, and capability.

### Core Argument

The company is not one thing. It is a bounded system organized to create, deliver, and capture value. Its outer boundary gives it legal and economic coherence. Its inner core aligns action, gets work done, and builds capabilities over time.

### Narrative Device

Use Hannah Schmidt as the recurring entrepreneur throughout the chapter. She begins inside a software development agency, unhappy and underused, with a passion for sewing and a growing conviction that industrial sewing and tailoring machines need better software. Her story should appear in each major section so the chapter feels like one continuous narrative rather than a stack of theoretical summaries.

### Proposed Flow

1. Open with the claim that most people misunderstand what a company is
2. Introduce value creation, delivery, and capture as the directional core
3. Explain the outer boundary:
   - legal boundary
   - economic boundary
4. Explain the inner core:
   - control system
   - operating engine
   - capability engine
5. Close by teasing the AI-native company and the one-human-many-agents model
