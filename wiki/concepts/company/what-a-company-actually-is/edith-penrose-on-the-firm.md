# Edith Penrose on the Firm

Edith Penrose moves the theory of the firm inside the company. In [The Theory of the Growth of the Firm](https://doi.org/10.1093/0198289774.003.0001), she treats the firm as a bundle of productive resources whose growth depends on how management deploys and recombines them.

## Sections

### Core Idea

Penrose's perspective matters because it makes the company more than a legal wrapper or contract bundle. The firm becomes a living accumulation of knowledge, managerial attention, and productive services that can be used in new ways. Growth is constrained not just by capital, but by the capacity to organize and absorb more activity.

### Why It Matters

This is a powerful bridge to an AI-native firm. If a firm's capabilities depend on how it deploys knowledge and managerial services, then agents may dramatically expand the productive services available to a single human-led company.
