# Legal Personhood and the Company

From a legal perspective, a company is a person-like entity created by law. That legal status allows it to own property, enter contracts, sue and be sued, and continue beyond the lives of its founders.

## Sections

### Core Idea

The legal definition explains why a company can act as a durable container for economic activity. In [Britannica's corporation summary](https://www.britannica.com/summary/corporation), the corporation is presented as a legal form with limited liability, share transferability, juridical personality, and indefinite duration. In [Britannica's legal personhood entry](https://www.britannica.com/topic/legal-personhood), legal personhood is what allows a corporation to participate in the legal system as an entity.

### Why It Matters

This lens does not tell you everything about what a company is, but it tells you why a company can hold assets, protect investors, and survive management turnover. It is the outer shell that makes the rest of the company possible.
