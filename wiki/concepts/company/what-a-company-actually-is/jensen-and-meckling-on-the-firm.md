# Jensen and Meckling on the Firm

Michael Jensen and William H. Meckling's 1976 paper [Theory of the firm: Managerial behavior, agency costs and ownership structure](https://EconPapers.repec.org/RePEc:eee:jfinec:v:3:y:1976:i:4:p:305-360) shifts attention from production to contracts and incentives. Their view is often summarized as the firm being a nexus of contracts.

## Sections

### Core Idea

In this lens, the company is not a seamless organism. It is a bundle of relationships among owners, managers, employees, creditors, and other parties. The hard problem inside the firm is not only coordination, but agency: how to make people act in line with the interests of those who bear the economic consequences.

### Why It Matters

This matters for your book because an agent-rich company will still face agency and monitoring problems, even if some agents are software. The company remains a structure for assigning incentives, residual claims, and responsibility.
