# What a Company Actually Is

There is no single universally sufficient definition of a company. The strongest way to understand it is as a stack of lenses: legal entity, coordination mechanism, governance structure, contract nexus, resource bundle, and managerial enterprise.

### Working Synthesis

The legal lens explains why the company can own property, sign contracts, and persist over time. Coase and Williamson explain why activities move inside a firm instead of being left to the market. Jensen and Meckling explain why the inside of the firm is full of contracts and agency tensions, while Penrose and Chandler explain why the inside of the company also matters as a place where capabilities, management, and learning accumulate.
