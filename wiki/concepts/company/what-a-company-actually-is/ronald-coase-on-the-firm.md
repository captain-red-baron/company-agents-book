# Ronald Coase on the Firm

Ronald Coase's central insight is that firms exist because using the market is not free. In his 1937 paper [The Nature of the Firm](https://chicagounbound.uchicago.edu/journal_articles/3171/), he reframed the firm as an answer to the costs of price discovery, contracting, and coordination.

## Sections

### Core Idea

Coase asks a deceptively simple question: if markets are so powerful, why is not all production handled through contracts between independent actors? His answer is that the firm replaces some market transactions with authority and direction when that is cheaper or easier than constant negotiation.

### Why It Matters

Coase is foundational for this book because he makes coordination cost the key variable. If AI agents sharply reduce the cost of coordination, then the boundaries and ideal size of the firm may change in a major way.
