# Structure, Roles, and Spans of Control

Companies are built through structure: functions, business units, reporting lines, and spans of control. Structure determines how work is divided, who coordinates whom, and how much complexity one manager can absorb.

## Sections

### Core Idea

Alfred Chandler's work on the managerial enterprise is central here because he shows that modern firms become powerful when administrative coordination scales. James March and Herbert Simon also matter because they connect structure to bounded rationality, communication, conflict, and participation. Structure is not just a chart. It is a way of controlling attention and coordination cost.

### Why It Matters

This page is directly relevant to the one-human-many-agents thesis. If agents increase the effective span of control of a human operator, then organizational structure may compress dramatically without eliminating the need for structure itself.
