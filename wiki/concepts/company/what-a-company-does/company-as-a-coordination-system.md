# Company as a Coordination System

One of the deepest things a company does is coordinate specialized activity. It decides which work should happen inside one system, under shared direction, instead of being left to many disconnected market transactions.

## Sections

### Core Idea

This page draws most directly on Ronald Coase and Oliver Williamson. Coase explains why a firm can be more efficient than constant contracting, while Williamson explains why some transactions are better governed through hierarchy than through markets. Chandler adds that management itself becomes a coordination technology once enterprises become complex enough.

### Details

Coordination is what turns scattered effort into a company. Without coordination, you just have tasks, contractors, tools, and intentions floating around without reliable integration. The company creates a center of gravity that allows work to happen in sequence, at scale, and under shared priorities.

This includes deciding what should be done internally, who depends on whom, how information flows, what gets escalated, and how exceptions are handled. In practice, many companies are far less defined by product than by their coordination system. The product is what the outside world sees. Coordination is what makes repeated delivery possible.

That makes this one of the strongest entry points for your thesis. If agents radically improve coordination, they do not simply automate tasks. They potentially change one of the deepest reasons companies have their current size, shape, and management layers.
