# Company as a Production and Delivery System

A company exists to turn resources into repeatable output. That output may be physical goods, software, services, media, logistics, or financial performance, but the underlying function is the same: convert inputs into dependable delivery.

## Sections

### Core Idea

Penrose is especially useful here because she treats the firm as a productive bundle of resources whose services can be redeployed. Chandler complements that by showing how large firms build organizational capabilities that make scale and repeated delivery possible.

### Why It Matters

Entrepreneurs often think about companies in product terms, but beneath the product is an operating engine. Understanding that engine is important if you want to ask which parts can be redesigned around agents.
