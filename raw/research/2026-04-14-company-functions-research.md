## Topic

What a company does.

## Notes

- A company coordinates specialized work that would otherwise be split across many market transactions.
- A company decides what to build, what to stop, where to invest, who is accountable, and how conflicts get resolved.
- A company turns knowledge and assets into repeatable production and delivery.
- A company creates a governance layer so objectives can be set, performance can be monitored, and incentives can be aligned.
- Simon and March help frame the company as a decision system under bounded rationality, not as a perfectly rational machine.
- Chandler adds the idea that companies build organizational capabilities that markets alone do not easily create.

## Working Synthesis

A company does four core jobs:

1. It coordinates.
2. It produces and delivers.
3. It makes and channels decisions.
4. It allocates accountability, incentives, and control.
