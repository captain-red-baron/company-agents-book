## Topic

What a company actually is.

## Notes

- A company can be described from at least three different angles:
  - as a legal entity with rights and obligations
  - as an economic coordination mechanism
  - as an organizational system with capabilities, routines, and management
- Coase treats the firm as an answer to the cost of using the market.
- Williamson extends Coase and frames the firm as a governance structure that is useful when contracts are incomplete and coordination is hard.
- Jensen and Meckling treat the firm less as a single thing and more as a web of contracts with agency problems inside it.
- Penrose shifts attention inside the company and sees it as a bundle of productive resources shaped by managerial knowledge.
- Chandler emphasizes the managerial enterprise: firms become powerful when administration, learning, and coordination scale better than arm's-length market exchange.

## Working Synthesis

A strong working definition for the book is:

A company is a legally bounded, economically coordinated, managerially organized system for deploying people, capital, knowledge, and assets toward a shared objective.
