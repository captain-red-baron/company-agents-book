## Notes

- A company is built in layers, not in one move.
- The legal layer defines the boundary: who owns assets, who signs contracts, who bears liability.
- The governance layer defines who can decide, who can monitor, and how power is checked.
- The structural layer defines roles, reporting lines, and spans of control.
- The operating layer defines processes, routines, and information flows.
- The capability layer defines what the company gets better at over time.

## Working Synthesis

If you stripped a company down to its essentials, you would still need:

1. a legal shell
2. a governance system
3. an organizational structure
4. repeatable processes and routines
5. capabilities that compound over time
