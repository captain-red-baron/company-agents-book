## Topic

What is the goal or purpose of a company?

## Notes

- The goal of a company is contested, not settled once and for all.
- Peter Drucker is the clearest practical voice for the claim that the purpose of a business is to create a customer.
- The stakeholder lens argues that firms should create value for stakeholders, not only for shareholders.
- Business Roundtable's modern formulation emphasizes delivering long-term value to customers, employees, suppliers, communities, and shareholders.
- For an entrepreneur-facing book, it is probably not useful to choose between "customer", "profit", and "stakeholders" as if only one can be true.
- A more useful synthesis is:
  - the company needs direction
  - that direction starts with creating and keeping customers
  - it must also generate durable economic surplus or it will not survive
  - it must coordinate stakeholders well enough to sustain the system over time

## Working Synthesis

For this book, the most useful framing may be:

A company is a bounded system organized to create customer value in a way that can be captured as durable economic surplus.

That synthesis keeps Drucker's customer focus, preserves the economic reality of profit, and leaves room for the stakeholder and governance questions that determine whether the company can endure.
